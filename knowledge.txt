[GENERAL WASTE COLLECTION]
Service details:
- Weekly collection from residential properties
- 120 litre standard bin provided

Recycling Program:
- Weekly kerbside collection
- 240 litre standard bin provided

- Items accepted: Paper, cardboard, plastic, glass, metal

WHAT TO RECYCLE:
- Cardboard and paper (newspapers, magazines, mail)

SECTION 5: RATES & PROPERTY SERVICES - EXPANDED
================================================================================

[RATES OVERVIEW]
Council rates explained:
- Annual charge on properties
- Funds Council services and infrastructure
- Charged on rateable value of property
- Due dates: Quarterly or annual
- Late payments may incur penalties
- Concessions available for eligible residents

RATE TYPES:
- General rate: Standard charge on property
- Municipal charge: Flat charge per property
- Supplementary rates: For property value changes
- Interim rates: For new property divisions
- Special rate: For specific programs/areas

[SETTING & CALCULATING RATES]
Rate calculation:
- Based on capital improved value (CIV) of property
- Property valuation conducted regularly
- Rates calculated as percentage of value
- Municipal charge: Fixed amount per property
- Total rate = (CIV × rate in $ per $) + municipal charge
- Different rates may apply for different areas
- Residential, agricultural, commercial, industrial rates vary

[RATE ASSESSMENT & VALUATION]
Property valuation:
- Conducted by state valuation authority (VRB)
- Used for Council rate assessment
- Determined by property value in market
- Reviewed regularly - not same as Council assessment
- Annual notice showing valuation provided
- Can be challenged through formal process
- CIV = Capital Improved Value

[PAYMENT OPTIONS & DUE DATES]
Payment methods:
- Online through Council portal
- Bpay (reference provided on notice)
- Credit card (may incur fee)
- Phone: (03) 5153 9500
- Mail: Cheque to Council office
- Direct debit (can be arranged)
- In person at Council office

Due dates:
- Quarterly: Usually March, June, September, December
- Annual option available
- Grace period: Usually 30 days
- Penalties: Added for late payments
- Check your rate notice for specific dates

[RATE CONCESSIONS & HARDSHIP]
Concessions available for:
- Seniors (Pensioner Concession Card holders)
- Veterans (Repatriation Health Card holders)
- Disability pensioners
- Health Care Card holders
- Low-income earners
- Eligible amounts vary by concession type

HARDSHIP ASSISTANCE:
- Payment arrangements available
- Rate deferral options
- Financial difficulty support
- Contact Council for discussion
- No shame in asking for help
- Eligibility criteria apply

How to apply:
- Complete application form
- Submit evidence of eligibility
- Contact: (03) 5153 9500
- Process: Usually 10-15 working days
- Backdating may apply in some cases

[DEBT RECOVERY & ARREARS]
If rates fall behind:
- Initial: Reminder notice
- Then: Demand notice
- Penalties: Added (interest and costs)
- Legal action: May be taken if unpaid
- Recovery costs: Added to account

RECOVERY PROCESS:
1. Reminder notice sent (30 days notice)
2. Demand notice if not paid (within 14 days)
3. Interest accrues on unpaid amounts
4. Costs added for recovery letters
5. Legal action may commence
6. Property sale/seizure in extreme cases

PREVENTION & SUPPORT:
- Payment plans available
- Contact Council immediately if issues
- Concessions may help reduce amounts
- Financial counseling available
- No further action if resolved

[SUPPLEMENTARY RATES & INTERIM RATES]
Supplementary rates:
- Issued when property value changes
- New property or significant improvement
- Property divided into multiple lots
- Assessed mid-year
- Billed for remaining financial year
- Apportioned fairly based on value change

Interim rates:
- Issued for newly created lots
- Applied from date of creation
- Amount calculated for relevant period
- Billed separately from main rates
- Term may be short (few months)

[EXEMPTIONS & SPECIAL PROPERTIES]
Some properties may be exempt:
- Government properties
- Non-rateable properties
- Community facilities (some)
- Religious institutions (some)
- Educational institutions (some)
- Health facilities (some)
- Exemption process available

APPLICATION PROCESS:
- Complete exemption application
- Submit evidence of eligibility
- Council reviews and determines
- Appeals process available
- Annual confirmation may be required

[RATE NOTICES & INFORMATION]
Rate notice contains:
- Property address and details
- Previous balance
- Current year rates
- Concessions applied
- Due dates and amounts
- Payment method information
- Contact details for queries

ACCESS & MANAGEMENT:
- Online portal: Check balance, pay bills
- Print statements
- Receive electronic notifications
- Update contact details
- Set up payment plans
- View payment history
- Download records

[VALUATION DISPUTES & APPEALS]
If you disagree with valuation:
- Review notice received from VRB
- First: Contact VRB (not Council) - they set valuations
- Evidence: Gather property information
- Formal review: Request within timeframe
- Appeal: To Valuation Appeals Board if needed
- Council can provide information about process

TIMELINE:
- Notice received: Usually May-June
- Objection deadline: Usually within 60 days
- VRB review: Takes several months
- Appeal Board: Further 3-4 months if needed
- Resolution: Usually within 6-9 months

[SPECIAL RATE SCHEMES]
Special rates may exist for:
- Specific neighborhoods or precincts
- Infrastructure improvements
- Special services
- Council determines based on benefit
- Affected properties charged additional
- Must demonstrate benefit to area

[RATES INFORMATION & SUPPORT]
For rates information:
- Phone: (03) 5153 9500
- Email: Via website contact form
- Online: Account portal
- In person: Council office
- Mail: Rate notice includes information
- Website: www.eastgippsland.vic.gov.au

WHAT TO HAVE READY:
- Property address
- Rate notice (if available)
- Account number
- Question or issue details
- Supporting documents if relevant

- Planning permit applications

- Building new structures

- Changing use of building/land
- Removing trees (some)
- Clearing land
- Dividing property into lots
- Some minor works may be exempt

[PLANNING PERMIT PROCESS]
Step 1: Pre-application advice
- Contact Council planning team
- Discuss your proposal
- Identify requirements
- Understand costs and timeline
- Phone: (03) 5153 9500

Step 2: Prepare application
- Complete application form
- Provide plans and specifications
- Details of proposed works
- Environmental information
- Heritage information if relevant
- Supporting documents

Step 3: Submit application
- Lodge at Council office
- Or submit online if available
- Pay application fee
- Council confirms receipt
- Provides assessment timeline

Step 4: Assessment & consultation
- Council reviews against planning rules
- May consult with other agencies
- Public notification (if required)
- Neighbor consultation (if required)
- Additional information requests
- Assessment takes 8-10 weeks typically

Step 5: Decision
- Approval with/without conditions
- Refusal with explanation
- Approval in principle (further details needed)
- Can appeal if refused
- Conditions must be met before work starts

Step 6: Commencement & compliance
- Notify Council when work starts
- Follow permit conditions
- Arrange inspections as required
- Final inspection before completion

[APPLICATION FEES & COSTS]
Fees vary by:
- Type of development
- Complexity of assessment
- Works costs estimated
- Processing time required
- Consultation requirements

TYPICAL FEES:
- Simple residential: $200-400

- Complex/large projects: $2000+

- Consultation notices: Additional cost
- Inspection fees: May apply

INSPECTION STAGES:

[PET REGISTRATION OVERVIEW]
Registration requirements:
- Compulsory for dogs and cats
- Annual registration required
- Lifetime microchip registration recommended
- Owner responsibility
- Renewal by due date prevents penalties
- Concessions available for eligible residents

REGISTRATION BENEFITS:
- Legal compliance
- Animal identification/recovery if lost
- Council support for lost pets
- Public safety management
- Animal welfare monitoring

[DOG REGISTRATION]
Dogs must be registered:
- Lifetime microchip required
- Registration fee: Varies by age and type
- Desexed/entire: Different rates apply
- Working dogs: May have different rates
- Annual renewal: Usually by March 30
- Renewal reminders: Sent 4-6 weeks before

REGISTRATION PROCESS:
1. Obtain microchip (vet or Council)
2. Complete registration form
3. Provide microchip number
4. Pay registration fee
5. Receive registration papers
6. Registration certificate valid 12 months

COSTS (TYPICAL):
- Desexed dog: $50-80
- Entire dog: $80-120
- Concession holder: Reduced rate (30-50% off)
- Puppy (under 3 months): May be exempted initially
- Dangerous dogs: Higher fee may apply

[CAT REGISTRATION]
Cats must be registered:
- Microchip required (like dogs)
- Annual registration required
- Desexed rate vs entire rate
- Renewal by due date
- Indoor/outdoor status noted
- Identification important

REGISTRATION PROCESS:
- Same as dog registration
- Microchip essential
- Form completion and fee
- Annual renewal reminder

COSTS (TYPICAL):
- Desexed cat: $30-50
- Entire cat: $50-80
- Concession holder: Reduced rate
- Kittens: May be exempted initially

[DANGEROUS & RESTRICTED DOGS]
Certain breeds have restrictions:
- Pit Bull type dogs
- Restricted breeds declared
- Additional requirements imposed
- Muzzling requirements
- Containment requirements
- Insurance requirements
- Registration restrictions

REQUIREMENTS FOR RESTRICTED DOGS:
- Desexed (in most cases)
- Microchipped and registered
- Contained securely (fenced yard)
- Muzzle in public (if required)
- Leashed at all times in public
- Liability insurance may be required
- Training certificate (some cases)

APPLICATION FOR EXEMPTION:
- Can apply for exemption
- Evidence required
- Assessment by Council
- Community consultation (some cases)
- Decision: Usually within 2-3 months
- Appeals available if refused

[LOST & FOUND PETS]
If your pet is lost:
- Notify Council immediately: (03) 5153 9500
- Report to local police (lost property)
- Contact local vets
- Post on social media and community boards
- Check shelters and pounds
- Leave microchip contact updated
- Door-knock neighborhood if safe

COUNCIL'S ROLE:
- Records missing pets
- Community notification possible
- Assist with recovery efforts
- Sheltering of found animals
- Reunition assistance

IF YOU FIND AN ANIMAL:
- Check for identification/collar
- Contact microchip registry (scan at vet)
- Notify Council: (03) 5153 9500
- Keep animal safe and contained
- Provide food and water
- Photograph for records

- Document incident details

[FIRE DANGER RATINGS]
Victoria's Fire Danger Scale:
- Low (Green): Safe day
- Moderate (Yellow): Fire can start easily
- High (Orange): Fire spread likely
- Very High (Red): Fires uncontrollable
- Extreme (Dark Red): Catastrophic conditions
- Catastrophic (Purple): Do not implement plan

DAILY MONITORING:
- Published by Country Fire Authority (CFA)
- Available online: www.cfa.vic.gov.au
- Television broadcasts daily
- Updated each morning by 11am
- Check before outdoor activities
- Plan accordingly

[BUSHFIRE SURVIVAL PLAN]
Preparation steps:

PROPERTY PREPARATION:
1. Clear gutters of leaves
2. Remove dead branches overhanging roof
3. Cut grass/vegetation away from house
4. Prune lower tree branches (2m minimum)
5. Remove climbing vines
6. Store firewood away from house
7. Clear leaves and debris from yard
8. Enclose open spaces under deck/house
9. Install metal screens on vents
10. Secure loose roofing

HOUSE HARDENING:
- Metal roof preferable
- Metal gutters and downpipes
- Enclosed eaves
- Tempered/safety windows
- Metal doors
- No external timber features
- Minimal external vegetation

EVACUATION PLAN:
1. Identify evacuation route(s)
2. Plan alternate routes
3. Know locations of evacuation centers
4. Practice evacuation drill
5. Have transport ready
6. Ensure family knows plan
7. Update contacts regularly
8. Have supplies prepared

EMERGENCY KIT PREPARATION:
- Important documents (copies)
- Photos of house/contents
- Medications and medical records
- Irreplaceable items prepared
- Cash (ATMs may not work)
- Change of clothes/sturdy shoes
- Sturdy backpack
- Supplies for at least 24 hours

[TOTAL FIRE BAN DAYS]
Total Fire Ban declared when:
- Extreme Fire Danger forecast
- Conditions support major fires
- Resource demands peak
- Usually late afternoon announcement
- Next day implementation

RESTRICTIONS ON TOTAL FIRE BAN:
- NO outdoor fires (any kind)
- NO BBQs (gas and electric okay)
- NO smoking in open areas
- NO machinery that creates sparks
- Existing fires must be extinguished
- NO hot work (welding, cutting)
- Penalties for breach: Up to $57,000 fine

[EVACUATION INFORMATION]
Evacuation ordered:
- Issued by State Emergency Service (SES)
- Via media, sirens, door knocking
- Listen to local radio/TV
- Follow evacuation routes
- Leave immediately
- Don't wait for official instruction if unsafe
- Go to evacuation center or safe location

EVACUATION ROUTES:
- Mallacoota: Inland route via Genoa

- Bairnsdale: Multiple routes inland

[DURING A BUSHFIRE]
IF EVACUATING:
1. Close all windows and doors
2. Turn off gas at meter
3. Leave lights on
4. Shut internal doors
5. Leave gates open
6. Leave ute/car in driveway
7. Take prepared kit
8. Follow evacuation route
9. Go to evacuation center
10. Do NOT drive through smoke

IF SHELTERING AT HOME:
1. Close all windows and doors
2. Shut internal doors
3. Turn off gas
4. Leave lights on
5. Fill bathtubs with water
6. Wear protective clothing
7. Stay inside
8. Listen to radio for updates
9. Be prepared to evacuate
10. Monitor embers

PROTECTING YOURSELF:
- Wear long sleeves and long pants
- Sturdy enclosed footwear
- Hat (hard hat if possible)
- Goggles or safety glasses
- N95 or P2 face mask if smoke
- Wet towels around windows/doors

SECTION 16: COMPREHENSIVE FAQ - FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS
================================================================================

Q1: What are Council's opening hours?
A: Main office: Monday-Friday, 8:30am-5:00pm. Closed weekends and public holidays. Phone: (03) 5153 9500. Emergency line available after hours.

Q2: How do I pay my rates?
A: Online portal, Bpay, credit card, direct debit, mail (cheque), or in person at Council office. Multiple payment options available for convenience.

Q3: What if I can't afford my rates?
A: Contact Council immediately on (03) 5153 9500. Payment plans, concessions, and hardship assistance available. Don't ignore the issue.

Q4: How do I register my dog or cat?
A: Complete registration form with microchip number. Required annually. Desexed rates lower than entire. Concessions available. Contact (03) 5153 9500.

Q5: What can I recycle in my bin?
A: Paper, cardboard, plastic (1-7), glass, metal, cans. NO bags, food, styrofoam, ceramics, light bulbs. Contamination causes collection issues.

Q6: How do I report a pothole?
A: Phone (03) 5153 9500 or use online form. Provide location and size. Priority based on safety risk. Usually 2-4 weeks for repair.

Q7: Do I need planning permission for my renovation?
A: Many renovations DO require permits. Contact Council planning: (03) 5153 9500. Pre-application advice available free. Better to ask than build illegally.

Q8: What's included in my rates bill?
A: General rate (property value based), municipal charge (fixed per property), and any supplementary charges. Includes Council services and infrastructure.

Q9: How do I apply for a planning permit?
A: Complete application with plans, submit to Council, pay fee, wait for assessment (8-10 weeks), receive decision. Council guides you through process.

Q10: What's the bushfire season in East Gippsland?
A: Peak: December-February. Extended risk: November-April. Year-round risk possible. Check fire danger daily. Have evacuation plan ready.

Q11: Can I burn garden waste in my yard?
A: Generally NO. Total Fire Ban prohibits. Year-round restrictions apply in most areas. Green waste bins collected fortnightly alternative.

Q12: How do I make a formal complaint to Council?
A: Contact Council with details. Use complaint form if needed. Acknowledgment within 2 days, investigation 7-15 days, response within timeframe. Appeals available.

Q13: Where are the library branches located?
A: Bairnsdale: 60 Nicholson Street (main). Lakes Entrance: Marine Parade. Orbost: Irving Street. Mobile library visits remote areas. Free membership for residents.

Q14: What are the swimming beach conditions?
A: Check daily conditions at beaches. Lifeguards at some (summer). Rips exist - swim between flags. Jellyfish possible (summer). Respect marine life.

Q15: How do I report animal welfare concerns?
A: Contact Council (03) 5153 9500 or RSPCA Victoria 1300 854 627. Describe concern, location, animal. Investigation conducted confidentially.

Q16: Can I have a septic tank on my property?
A: Possibly. Requires Council permit. Site assessment needed. Not suitable all areas. Alternative: Mains sewer or treatment system. Contact Council planning.

Q17: What's the penalty for littering?
A: Infringement: $75-150. Illegal dumping: $200-500+. Prosecution possible for serious cases. Clean community benefits everyone.

Q18: How do I appeal a rates assessment?
A: Contact VRB (Valuation Review Board) not Council - they set valuations. Usually within 60 days of notice. Council can provide information.

Q19: What support is available during bushfires?
A: Evacuation centers, emergency relief, financial assistance, counseling, community support. Welfare checks for vulnerable. Contact Council during emergency.

Q20: How do I find out if my property has planning restrictions?
A: Check property details on Council website or request planning report. May include heritage status, zoning, overlays. Contact Council planning: (03) 5153 9500.

Q21: What do I do if my bin wasn't collected?
A: Check location and weight. Verify collection day. If missed, report within 48 hours: (03) 5153 9500. Council re-collects at no charge.

Q22: Are there concessions for Council services?
A: Yes. Pensioners, Veterans, Disability pensioners, Health Care Card holders may qualify. 30-50% reduction possible. Apply with evidence. Annual renewal needed.

Q23: How does the Community Fireguard program work?
A: Group-based bushfire preparation. Training, planning, community support. Helps neighborhoods prepare together. Contact CFA for participation in your area.

Q24: What wildlife is dangerous in East Gippsland?
A: Eastern brown snakes (avoid), crocodiles (none here), sharks (rare - avoid dawn/dusk). Most wildlife harmless if respected. Report aggressive animals to Council.

Q25: How do I sign up for library membership?
A: Visit library in person. Proof of name and address required. Instant membership. Free for residents. Card valid 3 years. Children need parent signature.

SECTION 18: PROPERTY OWNERSHIP & LAND INFORMATION
================================================================================

[PROPERTY INFORMATION SERVICES]
Obtaining property information:
- Land titles (from Land Titles Office)
- Property valuations (VRB)
- Planning restrictions (Council)
- Zoning information (Council)
- Rates information (Council)
- Building/heritage info (Council)
- Environmental overlays (Council)

HOW TO ACCESS:
- Online Council portal
- Council counter inquiry
- Phone: (03) 5153 9500
- Land Titles Office: Direct
- Property search services: Available
- Real estate: May provide (transaction)

[PROPERTY BOUNDARIES & SURVEYS]
Determining boundaries:
- Current title deed shows boundaries
- Survey plan required (professional)
- Surveyor registered with Professional Surveyors board
- Boundary pegs/marks (may exist)
- Neighbor agreement desirable
- Council may have information
- Cost: $500-2000+ depending on size

SURVEY PROCESS:
1. Employ registered surveyor
2. Property inspection
3. Boundary identification
4. Calculations and measurements
5. Survey plan produced
6. Lodged with Land Titles Office
7. Certificate of title issued
8. Property records updated

[LAND TITLES & PROPERTY OWNERSHIP]
Certificate of Title:
- Official ownership document
- Issued by Land Titles Office
- Purchased in transaction
- Can request copy (small cost)
- Shows owner(s), mortgages, restrictions
- Essential for all transactions
- Updated when property changes hands

[ENCUMBRANCES & RESTRICTIONS]
Possible restrictions on title:
- Mortgages (lender interest)
- Caveat (legal claim)
- Easement (neighbor access rights)
- Covenant (use restrictions)
- Heritage protection
- Environmental restrictions
- Sharing arrangements

IMPACT ON PROPERTY:
- Affect use possibilities
- May require consent for changes
- Affect sale/mortgageability
- Disclosed at sale
- Can affect value
- May be removable
- Check before purchasing

[HERITAGE PROTECTION]
Heritage listings:
- Victorian Heritage Register
- Local Heritage List
- State significance or local
- Restrictions on alterations
- Demolition may be prohibited
- Restoration incentives possible
- Conservation mandatory

PROPERTY REQUIREMENTS:
- Alteration permits needed
- Heritage assessment required
- Professional advice recommended
- Specialist knowledge needed
- Grants may be available
- Tax incentives (some)
- Community input (major changes)

[ENVIRONMENTAL OVERLAYS & PROTECTION]
Environmental considerations:
- Floodprone areas
- Bushfire risk zones
- Coastal hazard areas
- Vegetation protection
- Wetland protection
- Native vegetation regulations
- Ecological value

IMPACT & RESTRICTIONS:
- Building limitations
- Clearing restrictions
- Floodproofing required
- Bushfire protection needed
- Vegetation clearing permits
- Environmental assessment
- Ongoing management requirements

[LAND USE ZONES]
Zoning determines allowable use:
- Residential
- Commercial
- Industrial
- Rural
- Conservation
- Public use
- Mixed use

PERMITTED USES:
- Specified in planning scheme
- Secondary uses possible (permit)
- Prohibited uses (legal issues)
- Home business (may be allowed)
- Neighborhood character
- Overlapping overlays
- Change of use (permit needed)

[PROPERTY COVENANTS]
Covenant types:
- Positive: Must do something
- Restrictive: Cannot do something
- Common: Building style, use, maintenance
- Can restrict development
- May be enforceable
- Removal possible (legal process)
- Can affect value

[EASEMENTS & SHARED RIGHTS]
Types of easements:
- Right of way
- Utility access (power, water, sewer)
- Drainage easement
- Shared access
- Heritage access
- Public access
- Private agreement

PROPERTY IMPACT:
- Limits your use of land
- Neighbor access rights
- Cannot block easement
- Utility maintenance necessary
- Affects buildable area
- May affect value
- Usually disclosed at sale
